# Board Resolution — Designation of Qualified Individual (Capital Wealth)

**Purpose:** Satisfy GLBA Safeguards Rule §314.4(a) requirement that the covered financial institution "designate a Qualified Individual responsible for overseeing and implementing your information security program and enforcing your information security program."

**Form:** Written consent of the Board (or board minutes if adopted at a regular meeting), counter-signed by the Qualified Individual.

**Filing:**
1. Original signed copy retained in CW's corporate records (Operations Manager: Lateesha Stevens).
2. Copy filed in Vault compliance workspace: `cw-federal-benefits/docs/compliance/01-board-resolution/signed/`.
3. Copy delivered to CW's E&O carrier and cyber-insurance carrier within 10 business days of adoption.
4. Cross-referenced in the Written Information Security Program (`../02-isp/ISP.md` §3).

## DRAFT — Resolution Language

### RESOLUTION OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS OF CAPITAL WEALTH

**Designation of Qualified Individual under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act Safeguards Rule (16 C.F.R. Part 314) and Designation of Chief Information Security Officer under 23 NYCRR §500.4 [if applicable]**

**WHEREAS,** Capital Wealth ("the Company") is a financial institution subject to the Federal Trade Commission's Safeguards Rule, 16 C.F.R. Part 314, and to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission's Regulation S-P, 17 C.F.R. §248.30, including the 2024 amendments thereto with compliance required by June 3, 2026; and

**WHEREAS,** the Safeguards Rule requires the Company to designate a Qualified Individual responsible for overseeing, implementing, and enforcing the Company's Information Security Program; and

**WHEREAS,** [if NY-applicable] 23 NYCRR §500.4 requires the Company to designate a Chief Information Security Officer with the authority and resources to implement and oversee the Company's cybersecurity program; and

**WHEREAS,** the Board has reviewed the qualifications and responsibilities of the proposed Qualified Individual;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED,** that:

1. **Designation.** [_NAME_] is hereby designated as the Qualified Individual under the FTC Safeguards Rule [and as Chief Information Security Officer under 23 NYCRR §500.4, if applicable], effective immediately.

2. **Authority.** The Qualified Individual shall have the authority to: (a) develop, implement, and maintain the Company's written Information Security Program; (b) require cooperation from all Company personnel and contractors in implementing the Program; (c) approve or deny security exceptions; (d) engage external experts and counsel as needed; and (e) report directly to the Board on cybersecurity matters.

3. **Reporting.** The Qualified Individual shall report in writing to the Board not less than annually (and more frequently as material developments warrant) on: (a) the overall status of the Information Security Program; (b) material risks identified and remediation plans; (c) material cybersecurity events; (d) testing results including penetration tests and vulnerability assessments; and (e) recommended program changes.

4. **Resources.** The Board commits to providing the Qualified Individual with reasonably sufficient resources to discharge these responsibilities, including budget for external assessors, security tools, training, and personnel as warranted by the Company's risk profile.

5. **Continuity.** In the event the Qualified Individual is unable to serve, the Board shall designate a successor within 30 days, and shall designate an interim acting Qualified Individual immediately upon the position becoming vacant.

6. **Documentation.** This resolution shall be filed in the Company's corporate records, the Company's written Information Security Program, and delivered to the Company's professional liability and cyber insurance carriers.

## QI candidate slate (for Mike's decision)

### Option A — Josh Cohen (Director of Marketing / BJX) — internal, immediate
- **Pro:** Already runs the data infrastructure (Salesforce, Vercel, GitHub, AWS planning), drafted this spec, has working knowledge of every system the ISP will cover.
- **Pro:** Zero additional monthly cost.
- **Con:** Marketing day-job is full-time; QI is a real responsibility that absorbs ~10-20% of a workweek during incident or audit.
- **Con:** Conflict-of-interest optics — same person who built the controls also signs off they're working. SEC examiners can ask why an audit-independent reviewer wasn't designated; defensible at CW's size but ask the question.
- **Con:** If Josh leaves, succession is unsourced.

### Option B — Fractional CISO (external) — ~$3,000-$5,000/month
- **Pro:** Independent of the build team. Cleaner exam optics. Has likely seen 10+ RIA cyber programs.
- **Pro:** Brings template ISP/IRP language; faster to a working program.
- **Con:** Cost.
- **Con:** Less context on CW-specific systems; ramp time.
- **Vendors to RFP:** Drawbridge, RIA in a Box (CISO services), AdvisorAssist, Compliance Risk Concepts, Vigilant Compliance.

### Option C — Hybrid — Josh as QI with fractional CISO retained for quarterly review + IR support — ~$1,500-$2,500/month
- **Pro:** Best of both — internal velocity + external independence on review.
- **Pro:** Matches CW's stage (post-PreciseFP, pre-SOC 2).
- **Recommendation.** This is the proposal embedded in the consolidated Mike email.
