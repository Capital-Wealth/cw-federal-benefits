## What You Can Do
We recommend that you take the following steps to protect your information:

1. **Review your account statements.** Carefully review the statements you receive from any financial institution. Immediately report any suspicious or unauthorized activity to the issuing institution.

2. **Obtain a free credit report.** You are entitled to one free credit report annually from each of the three nationwide credit reporting agencies. To order, visit www.annualcreditreport.com or call 1-877-322-8228.

3. **Place a fraud alert.** You may place a fraud alert on your credit file by contacting any one of the three credit reporting agencies. A fraud alert is free and lasts for one year.
   - Equifax: 1-800-525-6285, www.equifax.com
   - Experian: 1-888-397-3742, www.experian.com
   - TransUnion: 1-800-680-7289, www.transunion.com

4. **Consider a security freeze.** You may place a security freeze on your credit file, which prevents new credit from being opened in your name without your authorization. There is no charge to place or lift a freeze. Contact each of the three agencies above.

5. **Watch for identity theft.** The Federal Trade Commission provides resources on preventing and responding to identity theft at www.identitytheft.gov or 1-877-438-4338. You may also contact your state Attorney General's office.
