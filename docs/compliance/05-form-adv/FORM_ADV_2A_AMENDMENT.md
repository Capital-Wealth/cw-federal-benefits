# Form ADV Part 2A — Amendment Language for Vault Portal

**Purpose:** Update CW's Form ADV Part 2A (the "brochure") to reflect the new in-house Vault portal that replaces PreciseFP for client document collection and storage.

**Filing trigger:** Adoption of Vault portal is a **material change** to CW's information-handling practices because (a) NPI is now stored on CW-controlled infrastructure, (b) third-party processor identity changes, and (c) client information transmission methods change. Material interim amendment required promptly upon adoption per Form ADV instructions; integrated into annual updating amendment at fiscal year end thereafter.

**Filer:** Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) of CW (TBD — coordinate with current CCO). Filed via IARD.

**Delivery to clients:** Either (a) summary of material changes + offer of full brochure, or (b) full updated brochure. Delivered within 120 days of fiscal year end for annual amendment; promptly for material interim amendment.

## Privacy Policy — Material amendment language

**[Replace existing reference to PreciseFP and any prior client-portal description with the following.]**

### Information We Collect

In connection with providing advisory and insurance services, Capital Wealth ("CW", "we") collects nonpublic personal information about you from the following sources:

- Information you provide to us on forms or other documents — for example, name, address, Social Security number, date of birth, occupation, financial information, dependents, beneficiaries, and information necessary to evaluate and recommend financial products and strategies appropriate to your situation;
- Information about your transactions with us, our affiliates, or others — for example, account balances, transaction history, and payment information;
- Information you provide through our secure client portal at **vault.capitalwealth.com** (the "Vault"), including documents you upload such as identification, statements, tax returns, and pension information; and
- Information we receive from consumer reporting agencies, identity verification vendors, and other third parties engaged to verify identity and prevent fraud, consistent with our Identity Theft Prevention Program.

### How We Protect Your Information

CW maintains a written Information Security Program that includes administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of your nonpublic personal information. Specifically:

- We encrypt your information at rest using AES-256 encryption with customer-managed keys, and we encrypt your information in transit using TLS 1.3.
- Access to your information is restricted to CW personnel and service providers who require it to perform services on your behalf, and is governed by least-privilege controls and multi-factor authentication.
- We retain a designated Qualified Individual responsible for overseeing the Information Security Program.
- We engage qualified service providers (cloud hosting, database, document scanning, communications, monitoring) that are contractually required to maintain comparable safeguards and to notify us within 72 hours of any unauthorized access to your information.
- We test our safeguards through annual independent penetration testing and ongoing vulnerability scanning.
- In the event of a cybersecurity incident affecting your sensitive personal information, we will notify you in writing within 30 days of our determination that such information was, or is reasonably likely to have been, accessed without authorization, consistent with applicable law.

If CW elects to deliver a summary of material changes (rather than the full updated brochure), include:

> **Material Change Effective [DATE]:** Capital Wealth has launched a CW-operated secure client portal at vault.capitalwealth.com that replaces our previously-used third-party document collection service.
